\begin{abstract}
This paper analyzes the living-cost data from low-income families living
in Toronto, which is hosted publicly by the Open Data Toronto. It uses
information on household income, expenses, government fiscal transfers
and benefits to determine if low-income families are able to survive
financially in Toronto. It was discovered that expenses exceeded income
for most families (meaning low-income families are not able to survive
in Toronto), and rent, food costs were among the highest expenses. This
finding is important as it shows cost disprity in expenses, and guide
fiscal policymakers on how they can help low-income families with living
costs.
\end{abstract}
